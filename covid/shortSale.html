Why Consider a Short Sale?

If you are a homeowner and you owe more on your home than you can pay off, you may be facing the threat of foreclosure. You may be experiencing financial hardships due to job loss, death of a loved one, or even a global pandemic. Whatever the situation, it can feel like there no way out and it is only a matter of time before the bank takes the home you love and leaves you with a negative strike on your credit.
A short sale can help ease some of that financial burden. However, because your lender must agree to have your property sold for less than the amount you still owe on your loan, navigating the process can be tricky. That’s where an experienced short sale attorney can make all the difference.

How a Short Sale Attorney Can Help You

Experience Negotiating with Lenders

The short sale process can be risky for lenders as they must be willing to sell your property for less than the amount still owed on the loan. Because of this, your short sale application must be prepared with careful consideration and attention to detail. It must accurately represent your home’s value as well as a compelling reason as to why you have chosen to sell, demonstrating the hardships you are facing. These factors all contribute to your lender’s decision and having an attorney help you work out the details can mean the difference between your application being approved or denied.

Minimizing Added Costs

With a short sale, there are often additional expenses involved that homeowners may not have budgeted for. From closing costs to taxes, these expenses can add up quickly and put a roadblock in the process. When you retain the services of an experienced short sale attorney, part of our service is to minimize unnecessary expenses and negotiate added costs, helping you save money in the long run.

Having Someone on Your Side

Personal and financial challenges can take a toll on a person. Having to fight with your lender on top of it all can be the final straw for some. Part of our duties as your dedicated real estate attorneys is to represent your best interests and put your needs first. We negotiate with your lender on your behalf, handle paperwork and make the short sale process as smooth as possible for you. We protect your rights and take the stress of handling a short sale off your plate.
