A Compassionate and Caring Defense When You Need It Most

The probate process ensures a deceased person’s will is accurately interpreted and the involved assets are distributed as stated in the will. Dividing a loved one’s assets is already difficult following their passing, but in cases where the will is contested, that process becomes even more challenging. A probate attorney can help, easing the pressure off you and ensuring you receive what you are entitled to.

How Our Attorneys Guide You through the Probate Process

Ease the stress of Being Named an Executor

If you have been named an executor of a loved one’s will, making sure that person’s wishes are met can be stressful and emotional. A probate attorney helps take some of that stress off your shoulders by handling the bulk of the process with compassion and attention to detail. We will walk you through every step of the process from proving the will’s validity to assessing the value of any property involved. We are here for you.

Determine and Pay Debt, Taxes and Bills

When a person passes, their debt, bills and any taxes they owe must be paid before their assets can be distributed as outlined in their will. Your probate attorney can determine what those debts are and how they can be managed so that you may focus on yourself and your family during this time.

Ensure Assets Are Distributed According to the Will

Unfortunately, the process of dividing a person’s assets following their death can sometimes become ugly. Wills can be contested, and beneficiaries may disagree with the way assets are distributed. Ensuring those assets are divided as specified by your loved one in their last will and testament is perhaps the most important role your probate attorney will play. We will follow the instructions left behind by your loved one before passing and make certain you receive what is fair and what you deserve.
